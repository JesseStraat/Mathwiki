Algebraic Geometry

Algebraic geometry is the study of algebraic varieties, which manifest as zeros of a polynomial. Examples of these are the conic sections and elliptic curves. Algebraic geometry also concerns itself with special species of points, such as singularities.

Recommended Reading

  • Algebraic Curves and Riemann Surfaces, Rick Miranda
